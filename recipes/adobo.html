Adobo

Description

This chicken adobo recipe is a classic Filipino dish known for its bold, balanced flavors and simple cooking process.

At its core, it’s a braised dish where chicken is slowly simmered in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. The result is a rich, savory sauce with a distinctive tangy kick from the vinegar and deep umami from the soy sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (cane vinegar preferred)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 head garlic (crushed)
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1–2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional, for slight sweetness)
  • Salt to taste (optional)

Steps on how to cook

  1. Marinate (optional but recommended)

    Combine chicken, soy sauce, and half of the garlic. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for deeper flavor).

  2. Sear the chicken

    Heat oil in a pan. Remove chicken from marinade (reserve the liquid) and brown the pieces lightly on all sides.

  3. Add aromatics & marinade

    Add remaining garlic, then pour in the reserved marinade, water, bay leaves, and peppercorns.

  4. Add vinegar (don’t stir yet!)

    Pour in the vinegar and let it boil uncovered for about 2–3 minutes without stirring—this helps mellow the acidity.

  5. Simmer

    Lower heat, cover, and simmer for 25–35 minutes until chicken is tender.

  6. Adjust flavor

    Add sugar if you like a slightly sweet balance. Taste and adjust with salt if needed.

  7. Reduce sauce (optional)

    Remove lid and simmer longer if you want a thicker, richer sauce.
