Sinigang na Baboy

Description

Sinigang na baboy is a classic Filipino sour soup known for its refreshing and tangy flavor, usually made with tamarind as the base.

It is a comforting dish where pork is simmered with vegetables like radish, eggplant, and kangkong in a savory, sour broth that perfectly pairs with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork (belly or ribs)
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack sinigang mix or fresh tamarind
  • 6 cups water
  • 2–3 pieces radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2–3 long green chili peppers
  • Fish sauce (patis) to taste

Steps on how to cook

  1. Boil the pork

    In a pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork, onion, and tomatoes.

  2. Simmer until tender

    Cook for 30–40 minutes until the pork becomes tender. Skim off any scum that rises.

  3. Add vegetables

    Add radish and eggplant. Cook for 5–7 minutes until slightly tender.

  4. Add souring agent

    Stir in the sinigang mix or tamarind. Let it dissolve into the broth.

  5. Add remaining ingredients

    Add chili peppers and kangkong. Cook for another 2–3 minutes.

  6. Season

    Add fish sauce to taste.

  7. Serve

    Serve hot with steamed rice.
