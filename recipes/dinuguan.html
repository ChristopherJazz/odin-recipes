Dinuguan

Description

Dinuguan is a classic Filipino savory stew made with pork and pig’s blood, giving it a rich, dark sauce and bold flavor.

It is known for its slightly tangy taste from vinegar combined with garlic, chili, and spices, creating a hearty dish often served with rice or puto.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork (chopped into small pieces)
  • 1 cup pork blood
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2–3 tbsp vinegar
  • 2 cups water
  • 2–3 long green chili peppers
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Fish sauce (patis) to taste
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps on how to cook

  1. Sauté aromatics

    Heat oil in a pot. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.

  2. Cook the pork

    Add pork and cook until lightly browned and juices come out.

  3. Simmer

    Pour in water and let it simmer for 30–40 minutes until the pork becomes tender.

  4. Add vinegar

    Pour in vinegar and let it boil without stirring for a few minutes.

  5. Add pork blood

    Lower the heat and slowly add pork blood while stirring continuously to prevent curdling.

  6. Season

    Add chili peppers, fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Simmer until the sauce thickens.

  7. Serve

    Serve hot with rice or puto.
